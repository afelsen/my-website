June 2022 - August 2022

NASA Internship

Methane - a chemical resposible for 20-30% of global warming - is increasing in our atmosphere at an accelerating rate. The hydroxyl radical (OH) is a major methane sink, but its volitility makes it difficult to accurately measure.

This summer I utilized machine learning to improve detection of OH in the atmosphere. I leveraged artificial data from the GEOS model to predict OH concentrations from in-situ aircraft campaigns. I explored boosted decision trees and neural networks and analyzed their performance, reliability, and explainability. My mentor was Dr. Julie Nicely and my co-mentor was Dr. Bryan Duncan.

Learn more from my poster.

May 2021 - August 2021

M&M Printing Defect Detection

In the summer of 2021 I worked at Mars Inc. as a data science intern. Mars is known for its candy brands such as Snickers, Twix, and M&M'S, as well as petcare brands including Pedigree, Royal Canin, and Whiskas.

Over the 11 week internship, I developed a computer vision model to automatically detect printing defects on M&Ms. This can save many hours of manual quality control checks in the factory. Key accomplishments included the following:​
• Implemented a robust method to detect M&Ms in an image.
• Trained a PyTorch CNN to classify defective M&M prints.
• Introduced a new capability for creating synthetic data.
• Created a website in Dash that allows factory workers to upload images and analyze proportions of defects.

I had a great summer applying cutting-edge technology and learning from experienced data scientists on the team. I especially enjoyed having the freedom to find my own solutions to an important business problem.



August 2018 - May 2020

Fatigue Detection Model

In this unique 3 semester program at Binghamton University, I conducted computer vision research with a team of 6 other students.

We identified a subject's drowsiness level by combining auxiliary features (blinking pattern, yawning, squinting) and deep features generated by a CNN.

In November 2019, we presented our findings to students, researchers, and faculty.
